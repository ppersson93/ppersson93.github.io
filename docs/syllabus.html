Syllabus

I was at a party once, many years ago and described a piece of writing as "required reading" for being my friend, not as a litmus test, but because it had been so formative and because I found myself referencing it so frequently. It felt as though I didn't make quite as much sense to a person who wasn't familiar with it. Of course, this isn't true but it got me thinking about the idea of a personal syllabus. Pieces of writing that are so formative or important in one way or another to a particular person that reading them would be a way of getting to know that person. Below I've listed a few bits of my own personal syllabus.

Ben and Dara are in Love by Nancy Jo Sales

How to Kill a City by PE Moskowitz

Authenticity is a Con by Professor Andrew Potter*

My Gucci Addiction by Buzz Bissinger

On Heroin and Harm Reduction by Sarah Resnick

*Sadly, I'm unable to find a record of this on the internet. I read it in issue 8 of Vestoj magazine which is a truly remarkable publication on fashion, or, "sartorial matters" as they say.
